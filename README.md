## About

This guide is a short operational handbook for Instructional Associates.

The IA role focuses on:

- Tracking attendance
- Tracking homework submissions
- Sending student progress updates
- Giving short homework feedback
- Documenting concerns early
- Escalating issues to the lead instructor

This guide does **not** replace the student-facing course policies. It is meant to help IAs know what to do each day.

## IA Priorities

A good IA workflow is simple:

1. Track what happened.
2. Tell the student quickly.
3. Tell the instructional team when there is a concern.
4. Keep the documentation in Slack and the Course Tracker consistent.

> 💡 Students should never be surprised by their attendance status, homework percentage, or whether they are on track to pass.

## What IAs Are Responsible For

IAs are responsible for:

- Recording attendance accurately
- Checking homework submissions on due dates
- Updating the Course Tracker
- Sending homework feedback messages
- Sending weekly progress summaries
- Helping students during lab and project time
- Letting the lead instructor know when a student may need extra support

## What IAs Are Not Responsible For

IAs are not expected to:

- Rewrite course policy
- Make major grading policy decisions alone
- Handle serious student concerns without the lead instructor
- Manage every Slack channel in the workspace
- Replace the lead instructor during lessons

When a situation feels unclear, document what happened and ask the lead instructor.
